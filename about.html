About KASCOTE & KASCOTEN

The Kano Council of Traders and Entrepreneurs is one of the most famous platforms of entrepreneurs' organisations in Nigeria. It was established in 2009, and from the year 2010—exactly 13 years ago—the association made its landmark in Nigeria's socio-economic formation. Over 157,000 individuals are registered members of the association, owing to which different umbrellas of business communities including barbers, tailors, shoemakers/cobblers, farmers, and artisans' unions have joined KASCOTE.

In fact, KASCOTE encourages more individuals to venture into viable business and commercial activities. Hence, we developed a female-focused action plan aimed at mainstreaming women in business to convert them from the informal economy into a formal part of the economy. Typical examples include women producers of tea and spices, pomade, and tailoring services.

KASCOTE, as an autonomous body, works simultaneously with the Kano State government, especially the Ministry of Commerce. Its executive council is the highest policy-making body of the organisation. The President of the council is the Chief Executive Officer of the council. Executive members are often appointed or elected, and their nominations are usually presented during the Annual Meeting on election years (after a four-year term). Only registered members are entitled to elect the leaders.

For this reason, all interested executive candidates must be at the forefront through any one of the seven communities; and he/she must be a businessman/businesswoman or entrepreneur, and 1 woman must be among the executives during any election.

Our Communities

Members have seven (7) communities:

  • Commerce
  • Agriculture
  • Artisans
  • Miners
  • Youth
  • ICT
  • Women Entrepreneurs
  • Transporters

We collaborate with professional bodies, academia, and elder statesmen in organising annual events, including seminars, sensitisation sessions, symposiums, and workshops for the benefit of our esteemed members.

Our Objectives

The aims and objectives of KASCOTE include developing sectors such as commerce, small and medium enterprises, and the agricultural sector, as well as promoting entrepreneurial skills and activities across Kano and Nigeria at large.

Some of the various initiatives undertaken include:

  • Workshops and online programmes
  • E-commerce and trade missions
  • Committee interactions

Membership Benefits

  • Creating an enabling business environment to boost MSMEs
  • Promoting members' finished products and skills
  • Business registration to reinforce product and service integrity
  • Access to soft loans, grants, and assistance from governments or donors
  • Gathering trade data for improved business planning
  • Organising annual programmes, conferences, and meetings
  • Establishing local and international business relationships
  • Networking with successful professionals
  • Signing bilateral business MOUs with global institutions

Our Mission

We strive to rebuild world-class entrepreneurs with sharp business reasoning and provide a one-stop business information hub to identify multiple opportunities. Our goal is to establish a strong and dynamic facilitation centre that bridges the gap of expectations, aspirations, and yearnings of our beloved members.

Our Vision

We work round the clock to promote and develop economic wellbeing by providing quality services that enhance competitiveness. We aim to groom business-oriented visionary leaders who champion ease of doing business and effectively tackle challenges.

Trade Missions

KASCOTE actively promotes and attends trade missions, both nationally and internationally. These missions, often organised by government agencies, aim to explore international business opportunities. Our members regularly participate and benefit—especially with strong ties to Chinese companies and businesses that offer affordable opportunities for buying and selling products.

Our Target

  • Establish strong relationships with international business communities
  • Link with international donors and development partners
  • Collaborate with government on economic diversification
  • Educate and enlighten youth and women on modern business practices
  • Update members on global business trends
