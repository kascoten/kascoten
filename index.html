The City of Kano Profile Overview

Kano, the capital of Kano State in northern Nigeria, is a historic and vibrant metropolis with a population exceeding 4 million. Renowned as a commercial hub, it thrives on agriculture, trade, and manufacturing, with bustling markets like Kurmi and Dawanau. Steeped in Hausa-Fulani culture, Kano is famous for its Islamic heritage, the ancient Kano Emirate, and landmarks like the Gidan Rumfa and Great Mosque. The city hosts Bayero University and is served by Mallam Aminu Kano International Airport. While it faces challenges like urban congestion, Kano remains a cultural and economic powerhouse in West Africa.
